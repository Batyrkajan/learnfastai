Module: IoT Fundamentals

This module introduces the core concepts of the Internet of Things (IoT), focusing on how connected devices communicate, collect data, and enable smarter decision-making in various industries.

80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

What is IoT?

IoT refers to a network of physical devices, vehicles, appliances, and other objects embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity to exchange data over the internet.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • IoT connects everyday objects to the internet.
  • Devices collect and share data in real-time.
  • IoT enables automation and smarter decision-making.
  • Common applications include smart homes, wearables, and industrial automation.

Why It Matters:

IoT transforms industries by improving efficiency, reducing costs, and enabling new services. It also enhances personal convenience and quality of life.

Simple Takeaway:

IoT connects devices to the internet, making them smarter and more efficient.

How IoT Works

IoT systems rely on sensors, connectivity, data processing, and user interfaces to collect, transmit, and act on data.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Sensors collect data from the environment.
  • Data is transmitted via networks (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular).
  • Cloud platforms process and analyze the data.
  • Users interact with IoT systems through apps or dashboards.

Why It Matters:

Understanding how IoT works helps in designing efficient systems and troubleshooting issues.

Simple Takeaway:

IoT systems collect data, send it to the cloud, and provide actionable insights.

IoT Security

IoT security involves protecting connected devices and networks from cyber threats.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Weak security can lead to data breaches and device hijacking.
  • Encryption and authentication are critical for securing IoT devices.
  • Regular software updates help mitigate vulnerabilities.
  • IoT security is a shared responsibility between manufacturers and users.

Why It Matters:

IoT devices are often targeted by hackers due to their widespread use and potential vulnerabilities.

Simple Takeaway:

Secure IoT devices to protect data and prevent unauthorized access.

Why This Is Enough

This guide covers the foundational concepts of IoT, including its definition, how it works, and the importance of security. These principles provide a solid understanding of IoT's role in modern technology and its applications across industries.
