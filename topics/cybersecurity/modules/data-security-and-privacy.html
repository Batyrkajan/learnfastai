Module: Data Security and Privacy

This module explores the fundamentals of data security and privacy in the context of cybersecurity. Learn how to protect sensitive information, understand key threats, and implement best practices to safeguard data.

80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

Data Encryption

Data encryption is the process of converting plaintext into ciphertext to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Uses algorithms like AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) and RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman).
  • Essential for securing data in transit and at rest.
  • Requires encryption keys for encoding and decoding.

Why It Matters:

Encryption ensures that even if data is intercepted or stolen, it remains unreadable without the proper decryption key, protecting sensitive information from breaches.

Simple Takeaway:

Encryption is your first line of defense against data theft.

Access Control

Access control is a security technique that regulates who or what can view or use resources in a computing environment.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Includes authentication (verifying identity) and authorization (granting permissions).
  • Common methods include passwords, biometrics, and multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Helps prevent unauthorized access to sensitive data.

Why It Matters:

Access control ensures that only authorized individuals can access critical systems and data, reducing the risk of insider threats and external attacks.

Simple Takeaway:

Limit access to data to only those who need it.

Data Privacy Regulations

Data privacy regulations are laws designed to protect individuals' personal information and ensure organizations handle data responsibly.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Examples include GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act).
  • Require organizations to obtain consent, provide transparency, and secure data.
  • Non-compliance can result in hefty fines and reputational damage.

Why It Matters:

These regulations protect individuals' rights and hold organizations accountable for how they collect, store, and use personal data.

Simple Takeaway:

Follow privacy laws to avoid penalties and build trust.

Why This Is Enough

Understanding data encryption, access control, and privacy regulations provides a strong foundation for protecting sensitive information. These concepts address the most critical aspects of data security and privacy, enabling you to mitigate risks and comply with legal requirements.
