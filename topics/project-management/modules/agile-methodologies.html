Module: Agile Methodologies

Agile methodologies are a set of principles and practices designed to improve project management by promoting flexibility, collaboration, and iterative progress. This module will help you understand the core concepts of Agile and how they can be applied to manage projects effectively.

80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

Agile Manifesto

The Agile Manifesto is a foundational document that outlines the core values and principles of Agile methodologies, emphasizing individuals and interactions, working software, customer collaboration, and responding to change.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Four core values: Individuals and interactions over processes and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation, customer collaboration over contract negotiation, and responding to change over following a plan.
  • Twelve guiding principles that support iterative development, customer satisfaction, and team collaboration.

Why It Matters:

The Agile Manifesto provides the philosophical foundation for Agile methodologies, helping teams prioritize flexibility and customer needs over rigid processes.

Simple Takeaway:

Agile is about valuing people, collaboration, and adaptability over strict plans and processes.

Scrum Framework

Scrum is an Agile framework that organizes work into time-boxed iterations called sprints, with roles like Scrum Master, Product Owner, and Development Team to ensure effective collaboration and delivery.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Key roles: Scrum Master (facilitator), Product Owner (prioritizes work), and Development Team (executes tasks).
  • Key events: Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-ups, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective.
  • Artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, and Increment (shippable product).

Why It Matters:

Scrum provides a structured yet flexible approach to project management, enabling teams to deliver value incrementally and adapt to changing requirements.

Simple Takeaway:

Scrum helps teams break work into manageable chunks and deliver results quickly through collaboration and regular feedback.

Kanban Method

Kanban is a visual workflow management method that helps teams visualize work, limit work in progress, and optimize the flow of tasks.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Visualize work using a Kanban board with columns like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done."
  • Limit work in progress (WIP) to avoid bottlenecks and improve efficiency.
  • Focus on continuous delivery and improvement.

Why It Matters:

Kanban helps teams identify inefficiencies, reduce waste, and maintain a steady workflow, making it ideal for ongoing projects with evolving priorities.

Simple Takeaway:

Kanban is about visualizing work, limiting multitasking, and improving flow to deliver value consistently.

Why This Is Enough

Understanding the Agile Manifesto, Scrum, and Kanban provides a solid foundation for applying Agile methodologies in project management. These concepts cover the essential principles, frameworks, and tools needed to manage projects flexibly and efficiently, ensuring teams can adapt to change and deliver value consistently.
