Module: Kanban System

The Kanban System is a visual workflow management method designed to help teams optimize efficiency, reduce waste, and improve productivity in project management. Originating from Toyota's production system, it is now widely used in software development, manufacturing, and other industries.

80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

What is Kanban?

Kanban is a visual system for managing work as it moves through a process. It uses boards and cards to represent tasks and their progress.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Kanban focuses on visualizing work to identify bottlenecks.
  • It limits work-in-progress (WIP) to improve flow.
  • It emphasizes continuous improvement through feedback loops.

Why It Matters:

Kanban helps teams deliver value faster by improving workflow transparency and reducing inefficiencies. It is particularly useful in dynamic environments where priorities change frequently.

Simple Takeaway:

Kanban is about visualizing work, limiting WIP, and continuously improving processes.

Kanban Board

A Kanban board is a visual tool that represents the workflow, typically divided into columns such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done."

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Columns represent stages of the workflow.
  • Cards represent individual tasks or work items.
  • The board provides real-time visibility into the status of work.

Why It Matters:

The Kanban board helps teams track progress, identify bottlenecks, and ensure smooth workflow management. It fosters collaboration and transparency.

Simple Takeaway:

A Kanban board is a visual representation of your workflow, making it easier to manage and track tasks.

Work-in-Progress (WIP) Limits

WIP limits are constraints set on the number of tasks that can be in progress at any given time in a Kanban system.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • WIP limits prevent overloading team members.
  • They help identify bottlenecks and improve flow.
  • They encourage focus and completion of tasks before starting new ones.

Why It Matters:

WIP limits ensure that teams do not take on more work than they can handle, leading to faster delivery and higher-quality outcomes.

Simple Takeaway:

WIP limits keep teams focused and prevent overloading, ensuring smoother workflows.

Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)

Kaizen is the philosophy of continuous improvement, where teams regularly reflect on their processes and make incremental changes to improve efficiency.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Kaizen encourages small, ongoing changes rather than large overhauls.
  • It relies on team feedback and data-driven decisions.
  • It fosters a culture of collaboration and learning.

Why It Matters:

Continuous improvement ensures that teams adapt to changing demands and continuously enhance their workflows, leading to long-term success.

Simple Takeaway:

Kaizen is about making small, continuous improvements to processes for better results.
