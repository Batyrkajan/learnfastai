Module: Introduction to Business Analytics

Business Analytics is the practice of using data, statistical analysis, and technology to make informed business decisions. This module introduces the foundational concepts, tools, and techniques that drive data-driven decision-making in organizations.

80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

What is Business Analytics?

Business Analytics refers to the process of analyzing data to uncover insights, trends, and patterns that help businesses make better decisions.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • It involves collecting, processing, and analyzing data.
  • Uses tools like Excel, SQL, and visualization software (e.g., Tableau).
  • Focuses on descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analytics.

Why It Matters:

Business Analytics helps organizations optimize operations, improve customer experiences, and gain a competitive edge by making data-driven decisions.

Simple Takeaway:

Business Analytics turns raw data into actionable insights for smarter decision-making.

Types of Business Analytics

Business Analytics is categorized into three main types: Descriptive, Predictive, and Prescriptive Analytics.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Descriptive Analytics: Summarizes past data to understand what happened.
  • Predictive Analytics: Uses historical data to forecast future outcomes.
  • Prescriptive Analytics: Recommends actions based on data analysis.

Why It Matters:

Understanding these types helps businesses choose the right approach to solve specific problems and achieve their goals.

Simple Takeaway:

Descriptive tells you what happened, Predictive tells you what could happen, and Prescriptive tells you what to do.

Data-Driven Decision Making

Data-driven decision-making is the process of using data analysis to guide business strategies and actions.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Relies on accurate and relevant data.
  • Reduces guesswork and biases in decision-making.
  • Requires collaboration between analysts and decision-makers.

Why It Matters:

It ensures decisions are based on evidence rather than intuition, leading to better outcomes and efficiency.

Simple Takeaway:

Data-driven decisions are smarter, faster, and more reliable.
