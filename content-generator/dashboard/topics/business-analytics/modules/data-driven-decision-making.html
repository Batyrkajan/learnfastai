80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

What is Data-Driven Decision Making?

Data-Driven Decision Making (DDDM) is the process of using data analysis and insights to guide business decisions, rather than relying solely on intuition or experience.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • DDDM relies on accurate, relevant, and timely data.
  • It involves collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to identify trends and patterns.
  • Tools like dashboards, analytics software, and visualization tools are essential.
  • It bridges the gap between raw data and actionable insights.

Why It Matters:

DDDM reduces uncertainty, improves efficiency, and helps businesses stay competitive by making decisions based on evidence rather than guesswork.

Simple Takeaway:

Use data to make smarter, more informed decisions that drive better outcomes.

Key Components of Business Analytics

Business Analytics involves the use of statistical methods, predictive modeling, and data mining to analyze business data and generate insights.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Descriptive Analytics: Summarizes past data to understand what happened.
  • Predictive Analytics: Uses historical data to forecast future trends.
  • Prescriptive Analytics: Recommends actions based on data analysis.
  • Data Quality: Accurate and clean data is critical for reliable insights.

Why It Matters:

Business Analytics transforms raw data into actionable insights, enabling organizations to optimize operations, reduce costs, and improve customer satisfaction.

Simple Takeaway:

Analytics helps you understand the past, predict the future, and make better decisions.

Role of Data Visualization

Data Visualization is the graphical representation of data to help stakeholders understand complex information quickly and make informed decisions.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • Common tools include charts, graphs, heatmaps, and dashboards.
  • Effective visualization simplifies complex data sets.
  • It aids in identifying trends, outliers, and patterns.
  • Tools like Tableau, Power BI, and Excel are widely used.

Why It Matters:

Visualization makes data accessible and actionable, enabling faster and more accurate decision-making.
