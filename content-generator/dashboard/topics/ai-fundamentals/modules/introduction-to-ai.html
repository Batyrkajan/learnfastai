80/20 Study Guide - Key Concepts

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and make decisions like humans.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • AI systems can perform tasks like problem-solving, pattern recognition, and decision-making.
  • AI is powered by algorithms and data.
  • It includes subfields like machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision.

Why It Matters:

AI is transforming industries by automating tasks, improving efficiency, and enabling new capabilities like personalized recommendations and autonomous vehicles.

Simple Takeaway:

AI is about creating smart machines that can perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence.

Machine Learning (ML)

Machine Learning is a subset of AI that enables systems to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed.

The 20% You Need to Know:

  • ML uses algorithms to analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions.
  • It relies on training data to improve accuracy over time.
  • Common types include supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

Why It Matters:

ML powers applications like recommendation systems, fraud detection, and voice assistants, making it a cornerstone of modern AI.

Simple Takeaway:

ML is about teaching machines to learn from data and make decisions.

Why This Is Enough for Now
